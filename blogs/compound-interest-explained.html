🧮 How Compound Interest Works (with Simple Examples)

Published on July 2, 2025 • 4 min read

You’ve probably heard that compound interest is the “8th wonder of the world.” But what does that actually mean? In this post, we’ll break down how compounding works with simple, real-life examples—and why time is your greatest asset.

📘 What is Compound Interest?

Compound interest means earning “interest on interest.” Unlike simple interest, where you only earn on your initial amount, compound interest reinvests the earnings—so your money grows faster over time.

🔢 The Formula (Don’t Worry, It’s Simple)

The basic compound interest formula is:

A = P × (1 + r/n)nt
  • A = Final Amount
  • P = Principal (Initial Investment)
  • r = Annual Interest Rate (in decimal)
  • n = Times interest is compounded per year
  • t = Number of years

📊 Example 1: ₹1 Lakh for 20 Years

Let’s say you invest ₹1,00,000 at 12% annual return compounded yearly for 20 years.

Final Amount: ₹9,64,629

Total Interest Earned: ₹8,64,629

💡 You turned ₹1L into almost ₹10L — without adding a single rupee more.

⏳ Example 2: Starting Early vs Late

Imagine two friends investing ₹5,000/month at 12% CAGR:

  • Riya starts at 25 and invests till 35 (10 years)
  • Aman starts at 35 and invests till 50 (15 years)

Riya's Final Corpus (at 50): ₹55.3 Lakhs

Aman's Final Corpus (at 50): ₹40.6 Lakhs

Starting early made Riya richer—even though she invested less.
