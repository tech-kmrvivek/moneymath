👨‍👩‍👧 FIRE Planning for Families: School Fees, Marriage & More

Published on July 2, 2025 • 6 min read

Early retirement is challenging enough. Add kids, education, and weddings to the mix — and you need smarter FIRE planning. This post shows how Indian families can achieve financial independence while securing their children’s future.

👨‍👩‍👧 Meet the Family

  • Parents: Age 32 & 30
  • 1 child (age 3)
  • Combined monthly income: ₹1.5 Lakhs
  • Monthly savings: ₹50,000
  • Goal: Retire by 50

🎯 Key Future Expenses

Let’s estimate two major costs with inflation adjustment:

Child's Higher Education (Age 18)

₹40 Lakhs

Marriage Cost (Age 25)

₹25 Lakhs

These are future values, assuming 7-8% inflation over time. You’ll need to plan separate goal-based investments for these milestones.

💰 Family FIRE Number

Let’s estimate the total retirement corpus needed for the family.

Expected Monthly Expenses (at age 50): ₹70,000

Annual Expenses: ₹8.4 Lakhs

FIRE Number (8.4L ÷ 4%): ₹2.1 Crores

💡 This is just for retirement. Add ₹65 Lakhs more for child-related goals.

📈 SIP Strategy to Hit the Target

  • ₹30K/month SIP → for FIRE corpus (₹2.1 Cr in ~18 years @ 12%)
  • ₹20K/month SIP → for child’s education + marriage (₹65L in ~20 years)
📌 Total SIP: ₹50,000/month 🎯 Goal: ₹2.75 Cr across retirement + family needs

✅ Tips for Family FIRE Success

  • Don’t mix goal-based investing with retirement corpus
  • Use separate mutual funds or buckets for each goal
  • Factor in inflation for all long-term planning
  • Review and increase SIPs annually (step-up strategy)

📌 Final Thoughts

FIRE for families is possible — but it requires sharper planning, especially when kids are in the picture. Stay consistent with SIPs, separate your financial goals, and track your progress every year.
