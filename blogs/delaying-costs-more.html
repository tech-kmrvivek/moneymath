💸 Why Delaying Investment Costs You More Than You Think

Published on July 1, 2025 • 5 min read

We often postpone investing with the thought: “I’ll start next year when I have more savings.” But what seems like a harmless delay can actually cost you lakhs in the long run. In this blog, we’ll break down how compounding works — and why starting early is your biggest financial advantage.

🧠 The Power of Compounding

Compounding means your investment earns interest, and that interest earns more interest over time. The earlier you start, the more time your money gets to grow exponentially.

📊 Case Study: Riya vs Aman

Let’s compare two friends:

  • Riya starts investing ₹5,000/month at age 25 and stops at age 35.
  • Aman starts investing ₹5,000/month at age 35 and continues till 50.
  • Both get an average return of 12% annually.

At age 50, here’s what happens:

Riya's Corpus (invested ₹6L): ₹55.3 Lakhs

Aman's Corpus (invested ₹9L): ₹40.6 Lakhs

Riya invests less, for fewer years — yet ends up with ₹15 lakh more than Aman. That’s the power of starting early.

⏳ The Cost of Waiting Just 5 Years

Here's what happens if you delay your ₹5,000/month SIP by just 5 years, assuming 12% CAGR over 25 years:

  • Start at 25: Corpus = ₹95.4 Lakhs
  • Start at 30: Corpus = ₹53.5 Lakhs

That 5-year delay costs you over ₹41 lakh!

🎯 Start Small, Start Now

Don’t wait for the “perfect time.” Whether it's ₹500 or ₹5,000 a month, what matters most is time in the market — not timing the market.

Even if you're still paying off loans or just starting your job, start with whatever you can. Gradually increase your SIP as your income grows.

💡 Final Thoughts

Time is your most valuable asset when it comes to investing. Every year you wait, you lose the magic of compounding. Start now — even if it’s small — and let your money work harder than you do.
