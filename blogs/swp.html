While SIPs are for building wealth, a Systematic Withdrawal Plan (SWP) is for efficiently using that wealth to generate a regular income. It's a powerful tool, especially for retirees, that allows you to receive a steady cash flow from your mutual fund investments.

What is an SWP?

An SWP is a facility that allows you to withdraw a fixed amount of money from your mutual fund scheme at regular intervals (e.g., monthly, quarterly). Instead of redeeming your entire investment at once, you systematically withdraw small portions, allowing the rest of your money to stay invested and potentially grow.

Why is SWP a Smart Choice for Retirees?

  • Regular Income Stream: It acts like a pension, providing a predictable cash flow to cover your monthly expenses.
  • Capital Appreciation: Since your money remains invested, it continues to benefit from market growth, helping your corpus last longer.
  • Tax Efficiency: Withdrawals from equity funds held for more than a year are taxed as long-term capital gains, which is often more favorable than the tax on FD interest.
  • Flexibility: You can choose the withdrawal amount and frequency, and you can stop or modify the SWP as your needs change.
